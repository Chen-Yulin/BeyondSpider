# Kinetic penetration, angle ricochet, and unified penetrable targets

Cannon rounds were single-hit: a round raycast to the first collider, dumped its full `CurrentDamage`, and self-destructed. We replace this with a kinetic-energy penetration model. Every physical round — railgun slug and flak large round alike — is now a pure kinetic penetrator whose entire damage budget is its velocity term `D = mass · |v_rel|² · KineticDamagePerKE`; the flat caliber floor is gone and `KineticDamagePerKE` is bumped to compensate. Impact velocity is measured **relative to the struck block's own rigidbody** (extending ADR-0006's relative-velocity semantics), recomputed at every block. The governing identity: **absent a ricochet, kinetic energy lost equals damage dealt**, in the struck target's rest frame.

Each `FixedUpdate` a round sweeps `v·dt` and resolves the whole penetration chain in that one tick via `Physics.RaycastAll` sorted by distance, de-duplicated per part (one `NanoArmorBehaviour` = one hit, using the nearest collider — a part with several colliders is never double-counted). IFF is irrelevant to damage: any armor blocks and takes damage regardless of owner or team (team is a radar-display concept only), non-armor ship blocks are pure pass-through, and terrain is an impenetrable hard stop. At a block with remaining structural HP `H`: if `D ≥ H` the round **breaches** — deposits `H`, loses `H` of KE, and flies on with `D−H`; a breached armor block breaks off *and* turns permanently transparent to all future rounds, and any structural damage at all permanently disables that block's self-repair. If `D < H` the round did not breach: armor struck at a hit angle `θ > 45°` (from the surface normal) ricochets with linear probability `(θ−45)/45 · P_max`, reflecting in the block's rest frame, depositing partial damage and keeping only a small residual KE with the remainder dissipated (the sole exception to KE-equals-damage); otherwise the round embeds and dumps all remaining KE as damage. **Hit angle feeds ricochet probability only** — it never scales damage nor the breach threshold, so overmatch (`D ≥ H`) always penetrates no matter how oblique.

Armor and missiles are unified as **penetrable targets** behind one interface. Nano armor has a self-repairing integrity layer, a permanent structural layer, and can ricochet; missiles carry structural HP only and never ricochet (they embed or die). A heavy missile's existing `health` *is* its structural HP; interceptors gain a fixed structural HP of 5 and so become shoot-downable for the first time. A missile's mass is derived from `health` plus a new Warhead Charge stat — which also back-derives blast radius and blast damage — replacing the hand-set Threat Mass and Blast Radius sliders; the derived mass is surfaced in a read-only `MInfo` field.

**Status**: accepted

**Considered Options**

- *Keep the flat caliber floor as free first-hit damage* — rejected; it is not kinetic energy, so it breaks the KE-equals-damage identity. Dropped it and raised `KineticDamagePerKE` instead (all such numbers live in `SpaceBalance`).
- *Angle inflates the breach threshold (`D·cosθ ≥ H`) or scales per-hit damage (`×cosθ`)* — rejected. A clean penetration always costs exactly `H`, and angle governs ricochet alone; equivalent-thickness protection is expressed entirely through ricochet chance. Consequence accepted: sloped armor cannot stop an overmatching round.
- *Flak large rounds stay flat-damage and non-penetrating* — rejected in favor of unifying every physical round under the one KE model (flak gets a KE coefficient instead of its old `caliber·(1.6+energy)` flat damage).
- *Missiles as soft pass-through targets (take damage, never block)* — rejected; missiles are penetrable targets with structural HP, so a heavy missile can actually stop a weak round while a fixed-HP-5 interceptor is trivially blown through.

**Consequences**

- An entirely unarmored ship (no nano-armor blocks) is immune to cannon fire — only armor and terrain interact. There is likewise no friendly-fire or self-fire exemption: a round strikes your own armor if it sits in the muzzle line, mitigated only by muzzle-spawn clearance.
- Balance now hinges on several `SpaceBalance` dials that must be tuned together: `KineticDamagePerKE`, the flak KE coefficient, ricochet `k_keep` / `k_dmg` / `P_max`, interceptor structural HP (5), and the missile mass / warhead-charge → radius / damage curves.
- The "damage ignores IFF" rule extends to the laser as well: `LaserLanceBlock`'s `IsOwnShip` beam-skip is removed so beams strike own and friendly armor exactly like rounds, with the same muzzle-clearance caveat (a beam pointed through your own hull hits it).
- Any new round-vs-entity interaction should implement the penetrable-target interface rather than special-casing a collider type.
